📺 What are the benefits of hosting videos externally (e.g., YouTube embed)?
➜ External hosting (YouTube, Vimeo) saves your own server bandwidth and storage. They handle encoding, adaptive streaming, and deliver via global CDN — faster load for users worldwide. You also get robust analytics, captioning tools, and easy embedding with iframe. Drawback: ads, branding, or potential links to unrelated content. For full control and privacy, self‑hosting (like our <video> tag) is better, but you must optimize files.

⚡ How do you optimize images for faster page loading?
➜ 1. Choose modern formats like WebP or AVIF (smaller than JPG/PNG). 2. Compress with tools (TinyPNG, Squoosh). 3. Use srcset to serve different sizes based on viewport. 4. Lazy loading with loading="lazy" (as in our gallery). 5. Host on CDN. 6. Set explicit width/height to avoid layout shift. All these reduce bytes and speed up perceived performance.

🎯 In this demo: video uses a poster image, audio is external .wav (just for demonstration). For production, compress audio (MP3/ AAC) and use optimized video (h.264/h.265).
